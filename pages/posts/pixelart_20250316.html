Animation~

Aseprite/Libresprite has animation features that I hadn't used yet but I thought it'd be cool to use it to make the fox jumping animation I wanted to do. I wasn't really sure how I should incorportate that into the fox that follows the cursor and it occurred to me I don't have a site button yet! So, I decided I'd make an animated Fox Den Button™ ><.

For the cursor, I didn't really use much in the way of references or like even using the previous sprites as starting points (hence why the scale is pretty shit in places lmao). The animation timeline thing has onion skinning, a feature that shows previous and/or following frames to the one you're working on at a lower opacity. This made it really easy to draw following frames :D.

Animation Onionskinning

But anywho, that was fun also and I'm happy with the result and it's the same foxy guy so I just put it here. This was my first time making an animation and I definitely want to do more. I also hope you can be inspired to draw or animate something of your own ^^.

If you want to link to my site, click the Fox Den Logo in the right sidebar :3 It'll download the logo and copy the html you need to paste in your site >< (just make sure you move the image/adjust the path in the html to your needs).

I guess as a side note, being new to this, I did the animation horribly inefficiently. For anyone who maybe wants to try this sort of thing, I'd recommend roughly drawing out the major keyframes in just line art before anything else; I was completing each fox frame in it's entirety as I went which meant if I made a mistake, I put a lot of work into something that a) now had to be changed or discarded and b) due to the nature of this, had a cascading affect on the following frames of the animation. So I had like two instances where I had to move a bunch of frames to fix something small. Get the flow, positioning and pace of the most significant points of the animation right before moving on to doing all the frames, their detail, color and perfecting form (using the word "perfect" very loosely here lmao but you know what I mean).
